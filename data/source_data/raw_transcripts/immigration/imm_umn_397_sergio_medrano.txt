Description:
Sergio Medrano was born in Guatemala and lived there for 14 years. This video tells the story of his integration into the United States.

Transcript:

My name is Sergio Medrano. I am 37 years old and I am from Guatemala. I was born in Guatemala and lived in that beautiful country for 18 years. I had a beautiful childhood, but living with my single mother and two siblings plus being a low-income family drove us to immigrate to this great country of the United States. The trip was a bit difficult for me because I was so young and I left a part of my family in my country not knowing when I would return. When I arrived in the United States, I was reunited with my uncles, cousins and others who were waiting for me. The change was drastic and it was not easy despite the help of my family members. Integrating into a new culture, community and a new language was not easy, especially the language barrier. However, with perseverance and effort everything can be done. I managed to adapt to a new country and all the changes that come with it. The people who have definitely influenced my life are my mother, my wife and my children. They have been my greatest influence and have impacted my life in a positive way. Thanks to them I have learned to become a better person and they have encouraged me to continue in everything I have set out to do since I arrived to the United States. Without a doubt, one of the experiences that influenced me the most was the drastic change of residence. The drastic change of residence affected me in a positive way because due to the circumstances I was fortunate enough to be able to expand my horizons. I learned about the diversity of different ethnic groups and their many traditions and different ways of living. It has been a unique experience and has taught me a lot. Sharing my story allows others to know about myself and the struggles that I went through. I want others to see and cherish the opportunities that this country has to offer. As an immigrant I want to be an example for younger people to pursue their dreams and for them to become an example for the next generation to come. No matter the circumstances that you may find yourself in, always be positive. I think that the key to survival is to always have respect for others regardless of their age, appearance or color. All we need is love. Att. Sergio Medrano
