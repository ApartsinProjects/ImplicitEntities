12359_Story_Goldman__export_script.txt[1/25/21, 11:01:10 AM] During the late 19th century one of the Jewish community's greatest leaders was born in Russia. This leader was named Hymen Goldman. During Goldman’s childhood he faced the fear of being drafted into the Russian military, because in Russia when a male reached the proper age they could be drafted. This fear and the possibility of having a better life with more opportunities caused him to want to emigrate from Russia. While this was a lot for a fourteen year old to do all on his own, he persuaded his uncle and aunt to buy tickets to get to Canada where some of his other relatives lived. After time the aunt realized she was happy in Russia and wanted to stay but it left two pre-paid tickets remaining. His uncle then gave one of the tickets to a young boy of already military age and left one ticket open. This boy’s name was Malech and by the time he was given the ticket it was time for the boys to leave. Since there was no railroad that arrived at a port to go to Canada in Russia the two boys had to pass the border. As they were doing so they got caught and were put in jail. Luckily, they escaped by bribing. They got on a train to get to a ship and eventually arrived in Canada. When they arrived they had only 20 cents to their names. They then settled with Hymen’s family and got jobs working at a factory. Both Malech and Hymen hated their jobs and decided to quit to become peddlers for his other Uncle’s business in the country. While pedaling, Hymen received a letter from Aaron Goldman from Washington D.C.. Aaron was Hymen’s biological father that wasn’t there for him as a child. The letter exclaimed that Aaron had used his contacts in Europe to learn that Hymen had traveled to Canada and wished to see him. This angered Hymen because his father wasn’t there for him when he needed him but was also dazed since it was a way for him to get to America. Hymen went back and forth on whether or not to go, but before deciding, he decided to save his money in order to bring the rest of his family from Russia to Canada. The thought of his family being in Canada comforted him because if he didn’t like America he could still have a family to come back to in Canada. Hymen then prepared to go to America where he fell in love with Washington D.C. He quit his pedaling job to become a salesman and started his American Dream life. He began to learn English and make more money. But despite these improvements he missed his family but knew that these opportunities would benefit them. In America he found love and embraced his religion, Judaism. He joined a zionist party where he met his first wife Sadie. He married Sadie and started his family in America with three boys. With the birth of his kids his family that remained in Canada came to the U.S. All of these changes made Hymen realize that he didn’t want to be a salesman anymore and went into the tobacco business. He partnered with his father in law and founded the Standard Cigar and Tobacco Company, which was D.C’s biggest cigar distributor at the time. During this time Sadie died leaving him heartbroken, but it didn’t stop him from working. He continued to succeed in the tobacco industry and support his sons through college and the war. After some time had passed he remarried to a woman named Yetta. Throughout Yetta’s and Hymen’s relationship, Hymen began to embrace his religion again by helping the Jewish community in countless ways. For starters, he was the founder of the Jewish Community Center that is now a national organization that works to promote jewish culture and education. At this time that he founded the JCC, Israel was founded causing a forest to be named after him. This forest was next to the forest and monument of the John F. Kennedy Peace Forest since he had helped kindle peace between the two countries at the time. His charitable acts continued onto his son who donated a huge amount of money to the holocaust museum and was the president of the Jewish Community Council of Greater Washington; and chairman of the National Community 12359_Story_Goldman__export_script.txt[1/25/21, 11:01:10 AM] Relations Advisory Council. Overall Hymen Goldman's story is the ideal rags to riches story through the eyes immigration.
