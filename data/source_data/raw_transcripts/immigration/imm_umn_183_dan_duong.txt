Description:
Dan Duong was born and raised in Vietnam until the end of 2010. After she graduated from high school in Vietnam, she realized that she would be unsatisfied with the trajectory of her life if her family were to stay in their home country, so she convinced her parents that they all needed to move to America for a better future. She attended high school in La Crosse, WI and studied at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities.

Transcript:

Dan Duong Transcription My name is Dan Duong, and this is my immigrant story. While growing up in Vietnam, my dad had worked for VietNam Electricity Company (EVN), which is the only company that provides electricity-related services to the country. Because EVN is so large and they have such high demand for more workers, the company established its own university, which is the Electric Power University (EPU) to train students with skills they could bring to their future careers related to electricity. The realistic path for me after graduating high school in 2010 was to attend EPU, graduate after four years, and become employed for EVN because my dad had worked there for so long so that EVN would’ve given me a preferred admission into EPU as well as a lot of financial aid and a greater chance at being hired into their business. For financial reasons and because this was a relatively easy route for me so my parents wanted me to go in this direction, but I hated the education system in Vietnam. I hated how my high school was run, and my friends in college told me that that I would not have the right to choose what I want to study in college. Also, I know that if my family and I stayed in Vietnam, I would’ve for sure given up my passion for piano during college. On the other hand, the media and my family in America told me that my future would be bright in the U.S. So, when I was told that I had the chance to go to the States, my first thought was, like, Oh, I’m going to Berklee College of Music in Boston! That was my first thought, like, Okay, my dream is coming true! Because I dreamed of a better future, where I could have some say in what my career would be, and because I wanted the same opportunity for my sister, I convinced my parents that we needed to leave Vietnam. A few months after I turned 18, my family and I moved to Boston for a little while before settling in La Crosse, Wisconsin. My aunt, who sponsored us, decided that it would be best for me to learn the English language and the American culture by repeating high school. It was frustrating at the time because, as a high school student who was four years older than my classmates, I didn’t like how immature everyone was, and it was hard for me to communicate because everyone around me could only speak English, and I didn’t speak any English. It was also frustrating at home because my grandpa didn’t understand why I had to repeat all four years of high school in America because I was such a good student back in Vietnam, so he would question why it was taking me so long to graduate. Thankfully, toward the end of high school, I gained some confidence in my English and so school became more enjoyable. Finally, I was able to graduate with the class of 2014. Now, I’m studying Mathematical Biology at the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities, with the hope to become an orthodontist. As a young girl in Vietnam, I never would’ve thought that this would be my career choice because I was never given a choice when it came to careers, but I had the determination to chase my dream of a better future. Step by step, I’m on my way to achieving my goal now.
