Description:
Chiyou Moua was born in Bangkok, Thailand in 1990. He came to the United States as a refugee in 2004.  He lived in Colorado until 2010, where he went to school and graduated from high school.  His family moved to Minnesota and Chiyou got married in 2011.  He is currently a student at St. Paul College.

Transcript:

Chiyou Moua Transcription My name is Chiyou Moua. I was born in Bangkok, Thailand. In 2002, the International Organization for migration was in our Hmong village and they interviewed all of us to come to the United States. In 2004, I arrived in Denver, Colorado. I was 13 years old and I went to middle school for one year. Then I went to Arvada High School for 4 years. My freshman year of high school I started to play soccer. My soccer team liked me a lot and they liked how I played. I was the only Hmong guy on our team. There were a few Asians and Mexicans, but most of the team was white. They taught me to speak English. At the same time we were playing soccer and practiced together. My sophomore year, I made the most goals on our team. It was 29 goals and I represented my school to the conference award. I was the second highest score in the conference. I had the second Team All-Conference Award from the Jefferson County League from 2006-2007. This award is very important for me because it reminds me how to communicate with people from other counties. I think soccer was a great opportunity for people- immigrants like me who do not know how to speak English, because I learned English from the team when I played soccer. Sometimes it was hard to understand each other, but I always used body language to communicate. In 2010, my family and I moved from Colorado to Minnesota because we needed help with food stamps, more jobs, and relatives live in Minnesota. Life in Minnesota is going pretty well. I married my wife in 2011. I have happiness with my wife and family. Now, I have a part time job at the casino and I go to school. I really like it, because I use my English lessons from soccer to communicate with my boss, people in the college, and teachers. Finally, I will retain this award to remind me that I have been through a lot of things. I will keep motivating myself to move forward and be patient. I will not be shy. I will be confident in myself when I communicate with people. Then, one day, I can become successful.
