Description:
Luu Nhat Tam Ngyuen moved to the U.S. with her mom and dad in 2019. Unfortunately, her sister was not able to join them because of her age. Luu Nhat Tam is the only person who can speak English in her family.

Transcript:

Hello everybody! Today I want to share with you my family immigration story. My family has four people: my mom, dad, sister, and me. On another day in 2019 we received a letter from the U.S. immigration that told for my family to come to the United States. To this day, I remember that time my family was so happy. We were excited, smiling, and crying. Finally, my family's dream come true because we have to wait almost 13 years. We prepared many documents the U.S. immigrations need need. Suddenly, they canceled my sister's documents because she was over 21 years old. Another sad news with my family, we were so disappointed. We cannot control anything. We just followed steps by steps the U.S immigration want. On June 24, 2019, my dad, mom, and me came to The United States to fulfill our dream. My family had 2 statuses at that time. I remember a little sad for my sister, and a little excited for a new life. When we arrived at the airport, the first thing I saw is the big board with the word "Welcome to The United States of America" I thought in my mind "OMG it's my dream here". Suddenly, I became worried for myself, scared of everything because my world is open and they’re a lot of people but no Vietnamese. I looked around, I just saw the fast food, they didn’t have the Vietnamese food. Everything was so strange with me and my family. I changed my status excited and become worried. I talked with myself let’s calm down and you can do it because just only I in my family know English. I must take care of my mom and my dad. At that time, many things were happening with my family. The first thing is a different culture and different language. Everything is new with my immigration and my family. Sometimes we were thinking to give up and come back home. But we try our best and we are still here now. All the difficult and hardships we have to overcome so that in the future we will always remember the memory of the new flight in the U.S. Thank you for listening.
