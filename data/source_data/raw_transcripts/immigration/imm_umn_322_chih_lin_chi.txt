Description:
Chih-Lin (Amber) Chi was born in Taiwan in 1992. She grew up in Tai-chung and studied in Taipei. She came to the United States in July 2014 to study English so that she can eventually take over her parents' cycling factory.

Transcript:

Chih-Lin (Amber) Chi Transcription [Singing] Hi! My name is Amber. Today I’m going to talk about a family photo that I brought when I came to the United States. Let me give you some background about me. My Chinese name is Chi Chih-Lin. I was born in Taiwan, T’ai-chung, which has great landscape, a suitable temperature in the middle of Taiwan. I spent most of childhood in my hometown and then I moved to the capital city, Taipei, to finish my economic education. Before I graduated from university, I had received a text from my parents. My parents have owned a cycling factory in T’ai-chung, Dajia [District] for over twenty years. This is my decision to take over my parents’ business whose mainly export to Japan, [inaudible], and Europe. And I have to communicate with my customers by English. My English was very, very poor so I came to United States to take in the formal university instruction. I enrolled to Century College in Minnesota on July 2, 2014. I brought a family photo that we were celebration Chinese New Year on 2014. Look. Family time is pretty rare because my family’s only got two, three, or four days in a year. We make sure we do everything together. All the things we used to do when we were growing up. It’s very valuable, very precious time. I have got to say I felt so frustrated in the beginning. What I mean is, I put myself under too much pressure. I [was] always afraid to communicate with anybody because I think my English is not fluency. I don’t know how to talk. So, whenever I see my parents’ photo, it reminds me what they have been work so hard. If I just give up, my parents will [be] very disappointed. My family photo who encouraged me more struggle to accept the new culture and enjoy every day gives me boundless energy to overcome everything. I want to be their most proud daughter in the world. Since we are far apart, I’m deeply aware how much I love my family. This is just a photo. But the expression is far greater than any words. This represents a journey and a memory between me and my family. In order to pay them back, I have always to work hard so they can be proud of me. And my family’s photo is going to accompany with me to the next university in the United States. Thanks for listening!
