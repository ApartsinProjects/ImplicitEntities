Description:
Felipa Guevara was born in Mexico. Her mother was American and her father was Mexican. She eventually moved to the US.

Transcript:

Why I Came To The United States I came to the USA because my mother was an American. My grandma, her mother, went to live in Mexico when my mom married my dad. They had 10 children; 6 boys and 4 girls. We all lived in Mexico. When my older brothers grew up, they wanted to come to the USA. At that time it was allowed for children from an American mother and Mexican father to become American citizens. My mother got very sick during her last pregnancy. My grandma came over to visit and decided to take mother to the USA. My dad gave her permission to go, so my younger brother was born in the USA. Mom stayed in the USA and only went to Mexico to visit us for a month. She always got sick so she returned to the USA. Then she decided to bring the rest of the family to the USA. My older sister came to the USA, then it was my turn. It was hard for me because my dad had a small business as the milkman of the town. I was his helper. When my younger sister was able to help dad, my mom went to Mexico to bring me to the USA. She tried to cross the border with me after she had taught me some English words that immigration may ask. I was in front of the immigration officer and I forgot everything. I was not able to cross. They sent me back to Tijuana, Mexico, where I lived with my brother and sister-in-law. After a few months, I was able to get a 3 day pass to cross the border. I lived in Los Angeles, California with my grandma, mom, sister, and 2 brothers. I never went back to live in Mexico. I got married and had a family of my own; 5 children (2 boys, 3 girls) and one grandson. I got remarried and I also had a step son. After my ex retired, we came to live in Utah. My friend Ceci told me about the English class. I decided to take the class. It is not easy for me, but the teacher is patient and very helpful. So are the other students. I like the class. I have a good relationship with my brothers, sisters, family, and friends. Live one day at a time.
