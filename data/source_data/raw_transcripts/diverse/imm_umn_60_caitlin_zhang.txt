11425_Huiwen_Ni__export_script.txt[1/25/21, 11:01:13 AM] Caitlin’s family has been living in Minnesota for 5 years. Before that, they had a hard time making a living. The family decides to immigrate to the U.S. in 2006 when Caitlin was an elementary school student. After 6 years, their application of EB-3 is finally been approved. Carrying on the American dream, the whole family starts a brand new journey in mystic land which they never touched before. Their first destination was South Dakota. Caitlin found her senior aunt as the owner of the restaurant who sponsored the family. Caitlin’s family has no car and house at the time. The whole family lived in the basement in her aunt’s home. Her mom as the server in the restaurant used to be blamed by her aunt because of mistakes made by her poor English. Caitlin’s dad not only worked for the restaurant but also do all the housework for covering the rent: cooking, washing clothes for over ten people and picking up 5 children every day. Her aunt’s family didn’t welcome these new immigrants. They treated them as tools, instead of family. Caitlin’s family was discriminated and disrespected. After several months, Caitlin’s family was banished from that house. Caitlin and her little brother found and lived with her cousin Sherry in Virginia. They got on very well with each other because Sherry was born in Fuzhou and raised by Caitlin’s family there. Sherry’s parents moved to the U.S. 20 years ago for running a restaurant and left her child Sherry in Fuzhou because of limited time for taking care of her daughter, which is the common thing in most of the Fuzhou immigrants did. Caitlin’s parents worked for another restaurant in another city, haven’t seen her two kids for a year. Caitlin took care of her little brother like a parent for that entire year. She missed her parents, especially on the school events day when every American student invited their family. Because of lack of time for communication with classmates at school, she never hang out like American. Gradually, she was discriminated. Then the negativity from the school filled her. She started to cry emotionally that there is no fairness at all. It is not as simple as how much she pays, how good her grades will be. In order to receive the same grade as an American student, she would give up all her interests to look after her brother and use the little fragmentary time that she saved to study. Her classmates enjoy their high school event and fancy life, they study when they got time besides events, without any concern about living. Caitlin took after her brother without the support from the family. Her classmate did the babysitting for fun and pocket money, she did it for living. Fairness is based on a stable family but she never gave up and converted the pressures into impetus. She finally got 4.0 GPA and graduated top 10 from high school to Carlson. When the family planned to stay in Virginia for the long term and decided to run a new restaurant at the mall, Unfortunately, they were duped, by a Chinese old man, who has a strong connection between Fukien American Association in Virginia. They signed a fake contract with the original owner and the owner took a copy of the contract, without leaving any evidence from Caitlin’s dad. Her dad didn’t know the process at that time since he only understands a little English. Then her dad transferred up to eighty thousand dollars for future rent and decoration for that restaurant. Because of illegal contract, Caitlin’s family never owned this restaurant. The original owner took advantage of new decoration which paid by Caitlin’s family. Moreover, The owner threatened Caitlin’s family to move away. Otherwise, he would defame the reputation of Caitlin’s dad through his strong connection. Then every Fujian people there would know her dad asking for a. unreasonable money from the old man. Facing this unfairness, all the explanation from these newcomers was meaningless, comparing with an experienced landowner. Then they came to Minnesota! They took the loan to invest in a freight company cooperating with other Chinese people. Their family-owned company delivers the Chinese logistics goods from New 11425_Huiwen_Ni__export_script.txt[1/25/21, 11:01:13 AM] York, and store in Bloomington's warehouse for delivering to the Chinese restaurants in Minnesota, Wisconsin and Iowa. Facing to a better presence, all the unfairness and difficulties that Caitlin’s family has encountered are not that painful anymore. Caitlin said that for a brighter tomorrow, the hardships the family has encountered are worth, because they are part of growth. She believes all the difficulties are temporary. As long as they persist, disdain, unfairness, and deceives will all be gone eventually. The brighter future is waiting for her and her family.
