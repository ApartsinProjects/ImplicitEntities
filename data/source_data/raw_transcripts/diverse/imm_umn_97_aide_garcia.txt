Description:
Aide Esthela Garcia was born in Mexico City, Mexico. She first came to Los Angeles, CA to visit her father. Nine months later, she moved to Minnesota. After working there for nine years, she began her studies at Century College in 2014.

Transcript:

Aide Esthela Garcia Transcription Hi. My name is Aide. I’m from Mexico City. In 1999, and I came to United States to visit my father. When I made my suitcase, I made sure to bring my Virgen de Guadelupe with me because she stayed by my side since I was little. All my family members we are Catholic, and we are believing in the Virgin de Guadelupe. I decided to bring my image of Virgin de Guadelupe because my mother gave it to me. I believe in her because when I need any favor, I talk to her and I know she is listening to me. In Los Angeles, California, my father and I went together to church every Sunday to la Placita Olvera. Every year in Mexico on December 12, all Mexicans celebrate the day of Virgin de Guadelupe and everybody is going to the big church, to La Basilica de Guadelupe for celebrating. When went to Mexico to visit my mother and family, we’re going to visit La Virgin de Guadelupe and the Basilica and say thank you for everything she helped us.
