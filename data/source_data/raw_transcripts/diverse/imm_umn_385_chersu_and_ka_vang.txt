Description:
Chersu and Ka Vang have been married for over 46 years. In this video, they talk about living through war in Laos and adjusting to life in the U.S.

Transcript:

This is the story of my grandparents who were Hmong refugees from Southeast Asia. "My name Chersu Vang. I'm 72 years old, I'm a father of 5 and grandfather of 8." "My name's Ka, and I'm 69 years old and I live this country for 46 years." To the present day, my grandparents have been married for 46 years. They initially met at a young age in Laos. Chersu: "At first, when we still youth, I went to youth conference, so we met over there. And we just, kind of, barely know each other by name. Not a good relationship yet." Although they did not live in Laos for very long, they still carry lessons and traditions with them. Chersu: "Back in Laos, you know, birth country, I think it is taught us a lot to become a hard‐worker man. We had to work hard in order to earn money." Ka: "Yes, I was so pleased and very grateful I have lived in 2 countries, and I've learned a lot about my native country, like Laos. And when we came this country, then I have learned a lot. So, I speak 3 languages, and I know the traditional, like in my country that we don't have everything like this country, and when we came here we had everything so I had lots of experience." My grandparents were born in conflict, long before the US entered the wars in Southeast Asia. The fighting eventually became intense enough and close enough to their villages that they had to flee their homes. Chersu: "Yeah, it is very awful. Why is that? Because when the World War II was over, wars continued into our parents, and continued to us, and we had to move place to place and never had a peaceful life in a permanent place." Ka: "I lived a life of very danger because in the middle of the night we seen bullets fly all over our village. So we had to get up and run, run for life, so that was difficult. And since that, I so young, but we had to move one village for a couple of months, and then we had to move again because the war is keep going. So, we had very difficult. We could not have any permanent house or life to live." Coming to the US as refugees was quite the experience for my grandparents. Chersu: "When we came to this country, we had more opportunities to learn, to adapt to the new culture of this country which we had to learn how to live as an American." Ka: "For me, it like opened my eyes so I can see in this country they have more education, and I know that there's lots of love in this country, that love other countries and love to the poor, so it's so blessed that I have lived this country so we see lots of love from this country." Although my grandparents came here as refugees, the trials and tribulations, they earned their rights as citizens and became proud Asian Americans. Chersu: "I just want to let other people know that, I myself, when I came to this country, I had to work hard in order for living. Because, when I came to this country, I can see that the American people ‐ they not lazy at all. They work hard everyday. So, I'm one of the hard‐workers in this country in order to help myself, to raise my family, and to become a successful man." Ka: "For me, I want other people to understand for me that even I don't speak English very well, I don't understand very, but like my husband says we work hard. After we were at this country for 6 days, we work, we got a job, so we work even when we don't speak any English." I just wanted to take this chance to say how thankful I am for my grandparents and all the work that they went through, and for allowing me to tell their stories. I could not be prouder to say that I am their grandson.
