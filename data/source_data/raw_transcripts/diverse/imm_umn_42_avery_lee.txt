Description:
This story is about the narrator's parents leaving China to escape the daily political repression they experienced there. They moved to Canada and then the United States, having to start over their careers from scratch while starting a family.

Transcript:

This is my immigration story. So my parents are from China, and all of my ancestors are too. They had made the difficult journey to Canada to seek a better life. In 2002, my mom came from China and met my father on the way to Canada. My brother was born halfway through the journey, in 2004. My parents decided to come to Canada because there were better opportunities and more freedom. They moved to Toronto first because they had friends there. Most Chinese immigrants coming to Canada settled in places like Vancouver or Toronto because there was huge Chinese communities there. That's when my brother Tristan was born. After, they moved Montreal so that they could get a better education for both them and my brother. Later, my mom was offered a job at the University of Florida and we moved there for a year and had me in Gainesville. In 2008 they moved to Richmond Hill, for real estate purposes, and a couple years later we moved into Vaughan, and here we are now! My mother had to study at McGill University all over again because even though she was an engineer in China, and had masters degree, she had to re-apply for a license. My parents weren't earning much money as my dad hadn't gone to university at all and was a chef. In other words, being an immigrant was tough. Over the years, my parents had become more and more fluent in French, as they had moved to Montreal and it was a mandatory language, but they still weren't great at it. My mom had learned French at her University because it was in Montreal. My brother had actually gone to a daycare in Montreal, but even though his first language was French, his current grades for French are astonishingly low. While working on this project, I had asked my parents why they decided China wasn't good enough, and they had said, "In China, there was no freedom and no democracy". My dad is from China's capital: Beijing, and my mom is from eastern China in the province of Shang Dong. While researching and studying the journey my parents made to come here, I learned many things. For example, before, I hadn't even known that my mom was actually an engineer in China - completely different from her current job as a librarian at York University. I also never knew my brother Tristan's first language was French. Overall, I think this project has been a huge learning opportunity and I have learned so much.
