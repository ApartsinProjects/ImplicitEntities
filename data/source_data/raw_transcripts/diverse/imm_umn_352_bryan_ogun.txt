10870_Bryan_Ogun__export_script.txt[1/25/21, 11:01:11 AM] Hi, my name is Bryan I was born and I grew up in Nigeria specifically port-harcourt. My life there was not very good but it was normal because I had most social benefits but not enough always. The crime rate there was very high and living there peacefully was very hard. I left Nigeria because of the living conditions there wasn't very favourable. Like going to school was very expensive so it was a better option to live with my dad in the U.S. I came to the U.S. in June 2019. So basically cost of living is cheaper in the U.S. I came with my brother only. I left my mom most importantly because she wasn't eligible to come yet. And I left my aunts, uncles, cousins, friends, grandpa and my pets. I brought my good stuffs (my clothes, shoes and some traditional things which I use to remember my origins and where I belong). I left a huge amount of things in Nigeria because I was basically starting a new life in the U.S. I had mixed feelings when I was coming to the U.S. Though I heard so many good stories about America, but I was sad because of the people and things I left in Nigeria. For example, leaving my childhood life, my childhood friends, and everyone in Nigeria. My life presently is a lot different from life in Nigeria. For example walking on the roads of America is a lot more safer than the ones in Nigeria. Going to school is much more fun in the U.S. and lastly the cost of internet connection is cheaper. In conclusion my journey to the U.S has not been easy but I hope to adapt to the life in the U.S. and get used to living without my mom for some time in the U.S. Thank you.
