ORAL HISTORY OF GRACE ABBOTT, February 26, 1992, Sebastopol 


Arlene: Grace, could you tell me your name, address, your 
date of birth and place of birth? 

Grace: My name is Grace Abbott and I live at 445 Twin Lakes 
Circle in Santa Rosa. That's the Oakmont Village and I was 
born October the 20th, 1907 in Sebastopol. 

Arlene: Were you born at home? 

Grace: Well, at my grandmother's home, up on Florence Avenue. 
[possibly her aunt Florence Jackson's house. Mr. Jackson had 
a bakery here in town]. 


Arlene: Which house was that on Florence? 


Grace: I don't know the number of it, but it's the second 


house from the corner. 

Arlene: Near Healdsburg? 

Grace: No, near the other end, by Bodega. The next block 

on Florence Avenue on the right hand side. And it's the second 
house. It's still there. 

Arlene: It's not the Strout House? It's near the Strout House? 
Grace: Yes it is, it's just this side, about three houses this 
side. In fact, Erma Strout, one of the daughters of that family 

is an aunt by marriage of mine, so I'm familiar with the old house. 
Arlene: I wanted to ask you, you mentioned that your parents 

came here. I would like to ask where they came from, how they 

came and when they came. 

Grace: That's the grandparents? My grandparents were the ones 
that came here. The grandparents on my father's side, the Winkler 
side, he came here when he was sixteen. He came here from Kentucky. 
And he came primarily to go to the gold fields, at that time in 
1849. But after he got here, he decided that since he was a 
farmboy, he would prefer to farm rather than spend his time in 
the gold fields. Then on my mother's side, the Duer side, my 
grandfather came here, I'm not sure of the year, but it was 
sometime in the mid-1800's and he came from Missouri and he came 
first without his family. They didn't want to come. And then 
when he went back and brought his family a few years later, they 


came across the northern part of the United States and came 


(2) 


down from Oregon and settled in California. Whereas my father's 
father came across the plains driving a team of oxen and came 
over the Sierras and into Sacramento and down into Sonoma County.

wagon train? 


Grace: I've heard, in fact it's in our history, that my grand- 


father Winkler, did have some skirmishes with the Indians, but it 


was not of a serious [natureI. Well, it could have been but the

I've forgotten.
