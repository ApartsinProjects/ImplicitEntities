Description:
Yel Paw was born in Thailand in 2000. Her parents fled violence in Burma and lived in Thailand until the family later resettled in the United States.

Transcript:

file:///F/Immigrant%20Stories%20Transfer/Paw,%20Yel/Yel%20Paw%20Transcription.txt[7/6/2017 3:58:02 PM] My name is Yel T. Paw. I'm 16 years old and I'm came from Thailand in 2000, about 8 or 9 years ago. My parents were born in Burma. They went going through a lot of struggles to get to where they are now. Before I was born my parents had to accept a hard life from the Burman soldiers. In Burma there were lots of dangers. One day my parents decided to moved to the United States because in Thailand it was hard to find jobs, and it was hard to build a life there. They was worried about a lot of things. Because in my parents’ generation they couldn’t go to school. They had to work all day in the hot sun even though they wanted go school. My parents moved to the United States because they wanted their children to get educated and have more opportunities for good things. When I first came to the United States I struggled a lot because I didn't know anything about life in America. I was worried that I didn’t know anybody. I worried that people might laugh at me when I did something wrong. Right now my parents don't know any of the English words and they have to go work everyday. Every morning they work because they have to feed us and take care of us. It's hard for them. And that is why we are here. Now here we have a nice place, nice school, and everything's is like amazing. I feel like I'm in heaven.
