Description:
Enna Say was born in Sa La refugee camp in Thailand in 1999. In 2002 they moved to Mae La Oon refugee camp that is also in Thailand. In 2010 her family applied to be relocated to the United States, were accepted, and left in 2011.

Transcript:

My name is Enna Say. I was born in Sa La refugee camp in Thailand, on 1999. At night you could see many twinkling stars, which made the camp a pretty place to live. In 2002 we moved to Mae La Oon, another refugee camp in Thailand because a bad storm destroyed our home. When we arrived we built a bamboo house. My dad couldn't find a job. Many people left their family to work for Thai people. My father wanted to stay with his family. One day his friends told him he could make a lot of money by selling drugs. My dad started drinking and selling drugs made by his friends. My mom tried to make him stop, but he didn't listen. My dad tried to stop drinking because he wanted his children to do well in school. In 2003 and 2006 my brothers were born, making my dad happy, so he stopped selling drugs. In 2010 immigration people came to our camp and told us about America. My parents didn't apply because they thought our family wouldn’t be treated well. Later my parents talked with my aunt, in Minnesota. She said our family should come because there are a lot of opportunities. The next day my parents applied. After waiting 6 weeks we finally got our interview and were asked about our past and how we got to this refugee camp. They took everyone’s picture and said you have to see if you pass. Our family then went to Mae Sot for X-rays and shots. The X-rays showed my parents had TB so they had to stay for two more weeks. After waiting one month our family name was put on the board. Finally in 2011, our dream and hopes came true. We are going to America. We packed and Thai people drove us to Bangkok with other families. The food on the plane was disgusting, so I just watched the clouds. After the plane arrived in Minnesota we took a taxi to our new apartment, which was bigger than our house in Thailand. At school I saw many Karen people plus many other races I had never seen before. Two years later I could speak some English and I am learning more every year.
