This is the story about Allison Royer. Allison was born February 6th on an island called Dominica. As the famous African proverb goes, it takes a village to raise a child." In Dominica, that's just the way it was. Anyone in the village could correct you and execute corporal punishment without consulting your parents. These punishments were normal for him at school, on playgrounds, and even in church. Life was very structured, with no structure. Where he grew up they had no street lights, but they understood that they needed to be home and ready for bed before sunset. School days were the happiest days. Both of his parents were educators so he was incredibly smart for his age and he excelled in school. While in school he had two hours of work and 15 minutes of recess in the morning and the same thing in the afternoon with a 90-minute break for lunch. But not school lunch; he had 90 minutes to walk home, have lunch, and then walk back to class. After class he played many games with his friends which was the highlight of his day. Many of the games included fruit which had a direct correlation with the fruits that were in season at that time. As time passed on in his academic career around the 4th grade he finally was old enough to join his mothers class. But that was not something he wanted to do at all. So, he asked to go live with his father to avoid being in her class. He spent one school year with his father. According to him he went there with a third-grade education and returned with a third-grade education. It was not a very productive year, but he had lots of fun and learned many life lessons. Later, he move to St.Croix, a smaller island north of Dominica that is part of the United States. There was a minor difference in the culture but a major difference in how kids treated him after learning where he was from. This was the most difficult time of his life. Like everywhere else, being the new kid has multiple challenges. But soon he got involved with the local church, and before he knew it, he was having the time of my life again. Before he knew it, he had graduated from high school and was off to college. His graduation from high school felt so sudden that two weeks before his graduation, he was having one of those what's next?" conversations with his mother. She asked what his plan was, and he thought he could get away with just ‚Äúchilling at home‚Äù and giving himself a year to decide what he would do next. To that, she responded, ‚ÄúWhere are you going to 'chill' because you cannot chill here?" Meaning that he needed to make a decision sooner rather than later. Allison had two weeks to decide what he wanted to do and within those two weeks he decided to move to Georgia. Now his decision to move to Georgia was based off of several factors. The major factors being that he wanted to get away from home without going too far and he wanted to become an engineer. So within those two weeks before graduation he applied to as many schools as possible and eventually he started at DeVry in Atlanta. Georgia was a major shock to his system. The cultural shock was the least of it, though. The most noticeable difference was the weather. The heat was not something he had experienced, and the hot wind blowing was incredible and strange. It had never occurred to me that the wind could be hot because on the island it was cool. He went to Atlanta with the intention of getting his degree and going back home. But one month before his graduation, a secondary hurricane damaged the island so badly that his mother suggested that he just stayed in Atlanta. 30+ years later his life is still in Atlanta. He is married to his college sweetheart for over 20 years and they have three beautiful children and one grandchild. Now he working at really large company that allows him to travel to different parts of the world to work on really important projects. He is very happy that he decided to come to the states. And very grateful to have made so many meaningful connections along the way.
