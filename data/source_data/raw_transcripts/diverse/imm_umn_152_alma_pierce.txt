Description:
Alma Price was born in Mexico City, Mexico in 1976. After the death of her husband she decided to move to the United States with her two children. She now lives in Anchorage, Alaska.

Transcript:

I am Alma, I'm from Mexico, I have two kids, Fernando, he's 13 years old and Lucero, she's 6 years old. I was married 19 years but my husband died 4 years ago. After becoming a widow I decided to change my life and this included a change of country because the situation in my country is very harsh, there is a lot of insecurity, the government doesn´t help people, education system is deficient and salaries are very low. We have been living 15 months here, in this country, and it has been very difficult to adapt to the language and to the long and cold Winter. But this time has helped me to be calmer, more relaxed. I have been many things that I have never experienced, my children love snow and enjoy it as much as summer. Now I'm married to a good man who loves me and loves my children and although he doesn´t speak Spanish our relationship is very good. I want a better life for my children, better education, and better opportunities. At this moment my priority is my family and can work hard to get everything I want and be in direct contact with nature and forget the pollution and the traffic that exists in Mexico City. I was born in Mexico City and lived there for 40 years. The great majority of my work family was also born in the same city. I have to say that living in Mexico has only effected us in a positive way because we are all honest and hard working people without any type of bias. The story of how I meet my husband is very simple. It was here in Anchorage, Alaska. My uncle introduced him to me and it was almost love at first sight. The best experience we had as a family was the first time we visited the United States because we went to Disneyland, Sea World, Lego Land, and visited some cousins that lived in San Diego and Los Angeles. During this time we have been living in this city Anchorage, are more in like than before. We spent more time together, but we can’t stop missing our relatives. My children miss their cousins a lot and I miss my sister and my mom very much. I want my family to know that we are very happy to have been able to change life completely. There is a lot of opportunity, security, and nature near by living in the United States fulfills a dream I had since I was a child and now I have been able to achieve this. The last thing I would like to add in that is that my big dream is for my children to be happy, independent, and to start a career in order to work in the future. My personal dream is to work, have a house, and live the rest of my life with my husband Chris.
