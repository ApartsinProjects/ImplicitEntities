Description:
Shirley Chan was born in China in 1915. She died suddenly in San Francisco on September 4, 1943, shortly before her wedding.

Transcript:

This is all I know about Shirley Chan. She was born in China on August 5th, 1915 and died suddenly in San Francisco on September 4th, 1943… just days before her wedding and honeymoon. 70 years after her death, I got a call from her brother, a friend of mine, to say he had his sister’s trunk: her hope chest, with all the contents intact and untouched. He wanted to find a home for this rare and special time capsule. What can we surmise about the woman who picked the fabric and had these dresses made? Beautiful silk lingerie, robes, linens, intricate lace handkerchiefs? Even a comforter made of silk. Clearly she was a person with refined tastes. I made some calls to historical societies on his behalf without success, and finally asked if I could take it, not knowing exactly what I was going to do with it, but understanding that this is a snapshot of a time, of a woman, yet to live out her life. Bak chin gave it to me and later sent me this note: I understand completely your feelings of wanting to know a little more about Shirley because her life, like that of so many other Chinese women of her time, was impacted by drastic changes in Chinese social customs, encroaching world conflict, and being a member of a minority population in the US, endured overt discriminatory laws and outright oppression by ignorant and prejudicial government officials. Those were confusing and difficult times for everyone. He added, “I gave you Shirley's trousseau to do as you please with absolutely no strings attached.” In an odd way, it makes me feel more responsible. There’s a photo taken of her in 1937, the same year my mother arrived from China. And it struck me, that Shirley Chan could’ve been my mother.
