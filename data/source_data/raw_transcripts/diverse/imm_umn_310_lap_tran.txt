Description:
Lap Tran was born in Dalat, Vietnam in 1995.  He came to the U.S. when he was 16, in 2013. He is currently a student at Century College.

Transcript:

Lap Tran Transcription “Omamori” Hi! My name is Lap Tran and I was born in Dalat, Vietnam. I’m going to tell you the story of this. So, the story began when I was a kid, on my golden birthday, which [was] when I was five. My grandpa took me to dinner and played games. After dinner, he gave me this lucky charm from Japan and it’s called omamori. Because I was getting sick a lot when I was young, so my grandpa bought me this charm, and he believed that the charm would help me. On my way here, at a Japanese airport, I had a bunch of stuff with me, like, I brought everything with me because I had no idea what is America look like, so I brought everything, like Ramen noodles, two suitcases of clothes, water bottle, books, like, everything. So, at the gate, there was a lot of people there. I was looking for my ticket in my backpack but I didn’t see my lucky charm. And then I was worried, so I ran all the way back to check in, there you are, I was looking for it but it wasn’t there. I remember I stopped by the coffee shop for a minute, so I ran back to the coffee shop. I looked for it, but it still wasn’t there too. So, I saw the cleaning lady and was like, oh, maybe she saw it somewhere. And then I go to her, I ask her but she didn’t see it too. And I only have ten minutes left, so, and I was worried too, because that lucky charm was really important to me and that’s the only thing of my grandpa before he passed away. So, I only have ten minutes, so I give her my family and my email just in case she finds it somewhere. So, I walked back there. When I’m almost to the gate, I heard someone call my name. I turned around and saw the cleaning lady was there. She handed me my lucky charm and I was so surprised. I asked her where did she find it. And she said the cashier found it and gave it to her. So she ran all the way from the coffee shop, following me, all the way to the gate and then gave it to me. I was so happy, so I gave her the lucky charm that I made when I was in Vietnam. It kinda looked like this. This one is from my mom. She made it and gave it to me before I came here to wish me luck. I was so happy at that point. This lucky charm is very important to me and it has been with me for 15 years, more than 15 years, and it’s been with me at my highest and lowest point in my life. It’s so important. I lost this for a hundred times already, but it always comes back. This is the only thing that really reminds and connects me with my grandpa because he is the only one that really talked to me and told me to be myself when my parents were away. This lucky charm is just a huge part of my life that I’m glad I still have.
