You came back to California in 1959, right? Did you take all of your

He always told me he wanted to go home. My younger brother had come 
to attend a university in Pennsylvania to study from Kyoto University. 
We had planned to return to Japan together. But my brother decided to 
return by the way of Europe. My husband passed away during this time.

Do you consider yourself to be a true-hearted Japanese? 

I don't know. I have received many things from my experiences in 
America and I'm very grateful. However, I am Japanese at the core 
and can't be totally Americanized. I am just as you see me. 

Do you plan to return to Japan? 

No.

Yes, I do have a home in Japan, but I'm planning to remain in 
America. I've thought about living out my life in Japan, but after taking 
many things into consideration, I feel it's best to live near my 
children. 

Are you glad that you came to America? 

Yes, I do, especially for my children. 

As an Issei, what would you like to say to the younger generation? 
Wherever you are, Japanese will always be Japanese. Japan has 
positive characteristics which other countries don't have. Japan has 
her own unique culture. I would like to see the Japanese culture remain 
and continue on with the future generations. Mary feels like I do. 
She's working towards that goal now. I've always felt like this. 

Do you have any other thoughts you'd like to share with us? 

I'd like to see the positive aspects of the Japanese to continue on
