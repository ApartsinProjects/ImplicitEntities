Q@: Do you remember something of your childhood days?

After marriage, I came to this country but my husband 
worked for the railroad and we lived where there were no Japanese. 
My children started to go to church school and the minister 
asked me to come to church but I didn't have the courage to go

My husband was baptized in 1914 ina Catno.see Church. Tne

started to take English courses at Gonzaga College in Spokane. 
He said that once he made that decision, he took the path that he 
thought God wanted him to take and it was a great strength to him. 
I was quite moved by what he said but I did not become a Christian 
before WWII. 

After we entered the camp, we both started to go to 


church meetings regularly. After a while, the ministers kept 


coming to persuade us to receive baptism. I attended English 


classes in camp because my children thought I should learn 
English. One day, coming home from class, Mrs. Iwatsuki coe 
Hood River, Oregon) mentioned that it was about time I received 
baptism and become a christiane I felt something change in me 
so I went directly to a church leader in our ward and mentioned 
about baptism. He suggested not to wait until Sunday but 
receive baptism on Wednesday night at the prayer meeting because 
my daughter was leaving camp to relocate on Thursday. Thus I 
was baptized by Rev. Kitagawa at Reve Kuroda'ts living quarter 
witnessed by many friends. Il left camp soon after that. 

At that time, there was a shortage of railroad workers s 
my husband decided to go out and work. I waited for my son to

camp before the graduation ceremony Was held.

Washington D.C. 

My son attended college one year and was inducted into 
Army service. He was killed in action only 4 months after he 
was inducted. In those days, they were short of soldiers and 
boys were sent over seas after only 4 weeks of training. Then 
they were sent to the front line in 4 days. His last letter to 


us was written on Easter Sunday, April end, and he died on 


April 9th.

Losing my only son in the war was a great sadness to me

me not to go to work because I was not healthy. He wanted me 
to attend church meetings and prays He was 18 years old when he 


left. My son's words, I cannot forget.
