Do you remember about your father? 

Yes, I do. When I came to America I stayed with my

not in Japan. The only thing was that my father went 
the Russo-Japan War and came back victorious. We went 
to welcome him back. Well, there was a long parade and

I don't remember anything about my father from that time. 


How about your mother? 
My mother died a year after I was born. So I was

Was your step mother a kind person? 


No, she was not. 


Were you scolded by her often? 

No. It's not that she was mean or anything. I just

even then, it was not very intimate. When -‘you were

even if it were parents and child relationship. 


How far did you go in your education? 

School? I graduated elementary school, 6 years, and 
then 2 more years after that. Then, I went to one more 
year where they trained teachers. However, I could not 


complete that course because I came to the USA. 


Do you remember anything from elementary school or 
middle school? 
I was a good student, so when the entire student body 


gathered in the playground, I used to give command 


(for bowing heads to the principle.) Other thing was that 


teachers used pin my report card on the wall, so I was 


very proud. 


Do you remember your teachers? 

Yes, I do. When I was in elementary school, there was 
no special teachers. One teacher tought everything. I 
remember a teacher by the name of Tokan. He was a 
prodigy of a very famous teacher. However, he had such 
a new idealogy and he even demonstraited outside of 
school. So he could not advance to a good position. 

So this teacher was sent to a school in Taiwan. I 
really respected him very much. His thought was just 
too advanced for that time. He was not a socialist, 


either. 


What was interesting and fun events? 


Well, it must be a school picnic. We used to climb 


mountains. Or at that time there was this thing called 


"Chutai Undo". We used to go into a deep mountain with 
some’ food.’ It was’ a military training. Those were: very 
interesting. 


Do you have any sad memory? 
No, I can't remember off hand. However, when my father 
didn't send money to us from America, we had to suffer 


hardship. This was very hard. 


You must live with your step mother, right? 

Yes. However, my step mother left Japan for America when 
I was the third grade in elementary school. So I don't 
remember too much about her, either. So I lived with my 
grand mother, my father's side. I also had a sister, so 


three of us lived together. 


I also remember that my grandfather who was a carpenter. 
He was a good carpenter and he had 5 or 6 apprentices. 
However, he had a stroke and he could not work anymore and 
died when I was in the second grade. After that we, three 
of us, lived together. The only impression I have about 
him is that he was ill in bed, that's all. He could not 
work. My grandmother was a good mother. She was like my 


mother to me. 


When you were going to school, did you want to come 


to the USA? 


No. I never thought I would come to America. At that 


time the government was encouraging us to go to the 
South America. However, I didn't have any definite idea 


at that time. 


Then what was the reason why you came to the USA. 

Oh, I was summoned by my father. I really didn't want 

to come, though. However, my father sent me all the 
documents necessary for me to come to the USA. My grand- 
mother didn't want me to come, either. So I extended it 
as long as possible, but I had to come here after all. 


I was 16 years old then. 


What did you think America might be? 

Well, «I really didn’t think about it. I came ‘where 
because I had to come. I was very sad. I came with 
2 other boys, my classmates, and also a boy who lived

Nagasaki. The name of the ship was Tenyo Maru. However,

It must not been dammaged. Well, actually from Nagasaki, 
it went to Kobe, Yokosuka and then to Honollulu and then 


to San Francisco. 


How was it on the ship? 
It was very enjoyable. At that time there were many women 
who were picture brides. There were many of them on the 


ship. 


Did you have a chance to talk to them? 

Yes. They were marrying to men whom they never seen. 

So they were talking to each other about their husbands. 
They were all happy, though, on the ship. However, after 
they landed, many of them experienced tragedy. The 
husbands who came to pick them up were different from 
the picture, or they were older than women thought them 
to be. There may be those men who lided about their 

age, too. So I've heard about the tragedies of their 


lives. 


One of them cried all day long, because on the picture his 
profile was shown. So she could not see the other eye 
which was damaged. You couldn't help these things to 


happen because they were matched by pictures. It was 


parents or older brothers or sisters who made the decision 


for their marriage. There were also those who sent the 


picture dressed like respectable businessmen, even 


though they were manual laborers. I know there were many of 


those cases, too. Not all the people were like that, 


though. However, there were many cases like that. 


What happend to those women who were really disappointed? 
Well, hotel owners, or friends who came with them confor- 
ted them. I really don't know what happened to them 


after wards.

17 days. If I count the day I left Nagasaki, it would

aground.

No, I didn't make any. I had freinds from the village, so

What did you do? 
We run around the Deck, or talked. That's about all. 
We were seasick, too. I wasn't too sick, but I was
